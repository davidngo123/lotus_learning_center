10 Effective Ways to Practice English at Home
ESL Tips November 20, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

Learning English doesn't stop when you leave the classroom. Here are ten practical strategies to improve your English skills in everyday situations.

1. Watch TV Shows and Movies with Subtitles

Start with Vietnamese subtitles, then switch to English subtitles, and eventually try watching without any subtitles. This helps you learn natural conversation patterns and common expressions.

2. Change Your Phone Language to English

This simple change forces you to read English every day and helps you learn technology-related vocabulary naturally.

3. Practice Speaking with Family Members

Set aside 15 minutes each day to practice English conversation with your family. Don't worry about making mistakes‚Äîthe goal is to build confidence.

4. Listen to English Podcasts or Music

Find podcasts designed for English learners or listen to English songs. Try to understand the lyrics and write them down.

5. Keep a Daily English Journal

Write a few sentences in English each day about your activities, thoughts, or feelings. This improves your writing skills and helps you think in English.

6. Use Language Learning Apps

Apps like Duolingo, Babbel, or HelloTalk provide structured lessons and opportunities to practice with other learners.

7. Read English Books or News Articles

Start with children's books or simple news articles. Gradually increase difficulty as your skills improve.

8. Label Items Around Your House

Put English labels on furniture, appliances, and common items. This helps you build vocabulary for everyday objects.

9. Practice with Store Employees

When shopping, try to have brief conversations in English. Ask questions about products or practice polite phrases.

10. Join Online Language Exchange Groups

Connect with native English speakers who want to learn Vietnamese. You can help each other learn through conversation practice.

Pro Tip

Consistency is more important than intensity. Practicing 15 minutes every day is better than studying for 2 hours once a week.
